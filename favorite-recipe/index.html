Strawberry Oatmeal Cookies

scrumptious oatmealy strawberry goodness. Better than your grandma's cookies

Ingredients

  • 2 cups old fashioned rolled oats
  • 2 cups flour (spooned and leveled)
  • 1 tsp. baking powder
  • 1 tsp. baking soda
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1 1/2 cups freeze dried strawberries
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (melted and cooled for ~10 minutes (I just melt the butter in a large microwave safe bowl))
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2/3 cup brown sugar (light or dark, packed)
  • 1 large egg (room temperature)
  • 1 large egg yolk (room temperature)
  • 2 tsp. vanilla extract

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F and line a couple baking sheets with parchment paper, set aside.
  2. Pulse the oats in a food processor about 10 times. They should have some texture to them, not finely ground. Place the oats in a mixing bowl. Set aside for now.
  3. Add the freeze dried strawberries to the food processor, and blitz until it becomes a fine powder. A few little strawberry pieces are okay, but it should mostly be a fine powder. Set aside.
  4. To the oats, whisk in 3 Tbsp. of the strawberry powder, the flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt. Set aside.
  5. Add both sugars to the cooled butter and whisk until smooth. Be sure to break up any brown sugar clumps.
  6. Whisk in the egg, egg yolk, and vanilla extract.
  7. Add the dry ingredients into the wet and stir just until combined.
  8. Scoop cookies with a 2 Tbsp. cookie scoop (I actually make mine just a liiiittle smaller than 2 full Tbsp), roll into balls and place on prepared baking sheets. I like to bake 6 cookies at a time. If you make them smaller you can bake about 8 at a time. Keep the cookie dough in the fridge between batches.
  9. Bake for 9-11 minutes, do not over bake or they will be dry/crunchy instead of soft and chewy. Right when you take them out of the oven, place a circular cookie cutter or something circular that’s larger than the cookie so you don’t cut off any edges, and give the cookies a spin to give them a perfect circular shap
  10. Allow to rest on the cookie sheet for just a couple minutes, then transfer to a wire rack lined with parchment paper to cool.
  11. Enjoy!
